Color Harmony

Introduced 31 May 2026

General Information:

Color harmony describes a balance that Pue, the world, must maintain to remain steady and intact. It dictates the overall presence of color, as well as the ability to control it. Due to large-scale natural operations having a strong reliance on perceivable color as a multi-purpose material, it is crucial that Pue's color harmony can be maintained to a maximum.

Every general setting has a unique chromastone that contributes to Pue's overall color harmony. Chromastones interact with each other in a far-reaching invisible network, where pieces of harmony material may flow freely and survive. Each chromastone is actively maintained to keep its designated position within the network alive. As chromastones thrive off local activity and accumulation of harmony through certain rituals and practices, local overseers (Blanche, etc.) are present at each respective general setting to make sure that everything is in proper order, and that chromastones are fulfilled.

The Ultimate Rainbow Core is a large and inaccessible artifact, located in the center of Pue, that seldomly collects accumulated harmony material from the surrounding network of chromastones. Harmony material is collected once every 15 years. When a collection cycle ends, the core will grade Pue's performance. If the core returns a positive grade after collecting harmony from the surrounding network, all chromastones will experience a year-long buffer where maintenance is not crucial to maintaining a position within the network. During this interval, harmony would continue to flow without any problems. If the core returns a neutral grade, a much shorter buffer occurs and the people set out to work harder in the subsequent cycle. If the core returns a negative grade, it will return harmony material to the network, offer an overtime for extra accumulation, then return either one of two aforementioned grades or nothing at all depending on Pue's performance during the interval.

The amount of color harmony present in Pue has to reach a certain minimum threshold every year. If the threshold is not met, Pue's stability will be affected. The threshold requirements are reset after every collection cycle ends. Grading by the core will continue even if minimum thresholds are not met.

The core visually measures the extent to which color harmony is present in the world. Smaller versions of this artifact exist as acquirable charms, which can reflect the actual core's measurement of the world's color harmony in a way that can be seen.

It is important for the core to maintain proper health, as the stability of the chromastone network can be affected without effective care. Chromastones maintain the core by serving as active beacons to it, offering sustenance to it without heavy consumption of resources. Risks to its health can occur when a chromastone is severely tampered with, misplaced, manipulated, etc.
